Dover and Thanet Rights of Way Society

The Society was founded in 1974 to walk and protect the footpaths of East Kent, and we still regularly clear overgrown paths in the area.
We walk every other Sunday morning with a 10.30 start (10 o'clock in summertime), and normally cover 5 or 6 miles in 2 to 2 1/2 hours. These walks are either in or very close to the Dover and Thanet District areas, and are well supported. We also arrange whole day walks a little further afield on one Saturday each month. All start at 10 a.m., and are 10 to 14 miles long with a pub lunch en route. Evening social events are also organised during the year.
New members (and visitors to the area) are always welcome. The annual subscription is £5 per family, and includes two copies of "Waymarker" magazine, produced by our members.

St. Peters Costumed Walks

These are guided walks around the historic village of St. Peters near Broadstairs. The past is dramatically brought to life by costumed characters recreating days gone by.
During the guided tour visitors hear about life in the village over the past 400 years, they visit fascinating heritage buildings and places the casual visitor would never discover. Even local people may have a few surprises!
More Information or contact 01843 871200


Margate Civic Society

(includes Birchington, Cliftonville and Westgate)
The Margate Civic Society was founded in 1968. It is a registered charity. The society's purpose and aim is to encourage high standards of architecture and town planning in Margate and its environs. It works to stimulate public interest and care for the history and character of the area and to encourage the preservation, development and improvement of features of general amenity and historical interest.
Town Pride awards are given annually to those buildings which have been refurbished, cleaned or modified to enhance the appearance of the town. New, high quality buildings are also eligible for the award. The society monitors and reacts to local planning applications.
Evening meetings are held monthly between October and May at the Walpole Bay Hotel, Fifth Avenue, Cliftonville. The talks, usually illustrated, explore aspects of the locality in interesting and varied ways. Some afternoon meetings are also held. A newsletter is published four times a year.
More Information - Contact the Membership Secretary on 01843 228174


Ramsgate Costumed Walks

These are four walks around different parts of the town held throughout July and August. Each walk is led by knowledgeable local guides who introduce Ramsgate characters from the past - famous or not so famous. There is no set charge for these walks as we are all volunteers but donations to help meet the cost of the walks are welcome.

Rambleon

Rambleon is a not for profit, non-commercial site. It has been set up for walkers, ramblers and lovers of the countryside as a free resource to help you enjoy some of what England and Wales has to offer. It includes written walks, information on cycling, a list of events and festivals, walking related updates, as well as important information on health and safety and categorised lists of many other walking websites.
